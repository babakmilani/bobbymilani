Acquiring Danish citizenship is a major life step that requires meeting a stringent set of requirements and following a specific process. Citizenship is granted by **Act of Parliament** (a naturalization bill), making the procedure formal and lengthy, often taking two or more years from application to final ceremony.

Key Requirements for Naturalization

To be considered for Danish citizenship, you must meet the following major conditions set by the Ministry of Immigration and Integration:

  • Permanent Residence Permit: You must hold a permanent residence permit (usually for a minimum of two years by the time the naturalization bill passes).
  • Residence Period: You must have a lawful and continuous residence in Denmark for a **minimum of 9 years** (8 years for refugees or stateless persons). This period is shortened for those married to Danish citizens.
  • Self-Sufficiency: You must document **self-sufficiency**, meaning you haven't received public assistance (under the Active Social Policy Act or Integration Act) for a total period of more than four months in the last five years, and none in the last two years.
  • Language Skills: You must pass the **Danish 3 Test** (*Pr√∏ve i Dansk 3*) or an equivalent Danish language test.
  • Citizenship Test: You must pass the **Citizenship Test** (*Indf√∏dsretspr√∏ven af 2021*), which assesses knowledge of Danish society, culture, and history.
  • Criminal Record: You must meet the "good conduct" requirements, which include a strict ban on citizenship for those with certain criminal sentences (even suspended ones).
  • Declaration of Loyalty: You must sign a declaration of allegiance and loyalty to Denmark and comply with Danish law, which is often done digitally via **MitID**.

The Application and Process Timeline

  1. Digital Application: The application is submitted online using the digital solution on *Lifeindenmark.dk* and requires **MitID** for signing.
  2. Documentation & Fee: You must submit all required documents and pay the non-refundable application fee of **DKK 6,000 (2025 rate)**.
  3. Parliamentary Bill: If your application is approved by the Ministry, you will be included in a **naturalization bill** that must be passed by the Danish Parliament (*Folketinget*). This is only done twice a year.
  4. Citizenship Ceremony: The final step is attending a local **citizenship ceremony** where you sign a declaration and declare your loyalty to Denmark, often symbolized by a handshake with an official.

Dual Citizenship: Since 2015, Denmark permits **dual citizenship**, meaning you typically don't have to renounce your previous nationality to become a Danish citizen, though this is also subject to the laws of your country of origin.
